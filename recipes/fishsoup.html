Fish and Milk soup

Description:

I’ve said it once and I will say it again – Latvians love their soup for lunch, dinner or dessert. Funnily enough, we don’t commonly eat cereal for breakfast which could be classified as cold milk soup. One soup we love though is fish soup, especially made on bonfire with freshly caught fish right there on the bank of a river or a lake.

Ingredients:

  • 1kg/2lbs of whole fish, fish heads, bones etc.
  • Water
  • 1 onion, bay leaves, peppercorns, 1 carrot, couple stalks celery - for the stock
  • 6 medium potatoes
  • 0.5L/1pt milk
  • 1 bunch of dill
  • Sour cream to serve

Steps:

  1. Place fish in a large pot and add just enough water to cover the fish.
  2. Bring the pot to the boil, skim any scum from the pot.
  3. Add onion, carrot, bay leaves, peppercorns. Simmer the stock for at least an hour.
  4. Remove fish from the pot, reserve.
  5. Drain the stock through a fine sieve. There will be enough stock for several batches of soup.
  6. Pour 1 litre of stock in a smaller pot.
  7. Peel and cube potatoes. Add to stock. Boil the potates until they are soft.
  8. Meanwhile clean the fish from bones.
  9. When potatoes are soft, add milk to the pot and bring to simmer. Remove from the heat, stir in finely chopped dill and add fish.
  10. Serve with sour cream and rye bread.
